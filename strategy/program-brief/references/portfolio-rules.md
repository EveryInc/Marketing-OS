# Portfolio Rules

Sequence Marketing programs by likely business value, speed to learning, cost, team
capacity, and strategic importance.

## Default order

1. Programs with a direct, measurable free-subscriber path.
2. Programs that can create repeatable authority or Consulting demand.
3. Production-heavy media franchises after capacity exists.
4. Expensive experiential or awareness bets after the relevant product, sponsor, and
   operating gates clear.

## Capacity

- Run one high-effort build and one lightweight pilot at a time.
- Do not approve a portfolio whose simultaneous operating load exceeds the named team.
- Separate approval of an idea from release of its full budget.
- Stage later phases behind evidence from earlier phases.

## Evaluation

Acquisition programs compete on subscriber quality and economics. Authority programs
compete on the strength of the declared outcome, business path, and repeatability. A
program can be strategically valuable without winning a last-click contest, but it
cannot scale without a defined reason.
