# Information Hierarchy

## Executive reading order

1. **Decision or argument:** what leadership is being asked to believe or approve.
2. **Why now:** the company gap, opportunity, or risk.
3. **What changes:** the strategy, audience movement, or program.
4. **How success is measured:** the primary metric and business connection.
5. **Who owns it:** owner, support, and approval.
6. **What it requires:** budget, headcount, access, or capacity.
7. **What happens next:** timing and decision gate.

## Supporting material

Use short tables when they clarify audience differences, ownership, budgets, or
before-and-after positioning. Keep methodology, research detail, creative examples, and
program mechanics in the full strategy unless one is necessary to understand the
decision.

## Visual hierarchy

- Lead with a declarative title and one clear framing sentence.
- Use headings to show the decision path, not to decorate the page.
- Keep paragraphs short and use bullets for parallel facts.
- Put the budget after the strategy and measurement unless the decision is explicitly
  budget-first.
