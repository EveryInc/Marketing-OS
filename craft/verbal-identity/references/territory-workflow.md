# Verbal Territory Workflow

## Interview

Ask one question at a time:

- What truth does the brand insist on?
- What should the customer feel more capable of after contact?
- What can the brand say credibly that competitors cannot?
- Which existing piece of writing feels most like the brand? Why?
- Which writing feels successful but wrong for the brand?
- How knowledgeable can the voice assume the audience is?
- Where should the brand challenge, reassure, teach, sell, or entertain?
- Which words, tones, or cultural signals have become category wallpaper?

## Territory format

For each of the two territories, provide:

- Name and governing idea
- Strategic connection
- Functional or emotional lead
- Relationship to the audience
- Three or four behavioral traits
- Default cadence and tonal range
- Signature language moves
- Words to reach for and avoid
- Homepage, email, product, and social examples
- Failure mode and recuperation risk

The functional-led option must still have emotion. The emotional-led option must still
communicate product and proof. The distinction is which element organizes the system.

## Decision

Recommend one territory. The human may approve, reject, or synthesize. A synthesis needs
a stated organizing principle; combining favorite lines from both is not a strategy.
