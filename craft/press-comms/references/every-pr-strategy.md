# Every PR Strategy

## Objective

Make Every a recurring expert source on AI and work, led by Dan Shipper, while using
tentpole launches, research, partnerships, and company practitioners to broaden the
company's authority.

## Spokespeople

- **Dan Shipper:** priority voice for broad audiences; leading expert on AI and work,
  known for philosophical takes, accessible explainers, and deep working knowledge.
- **Brandon Gell and Kate Lee:** company, Editorial, and business stories where relevant.
- **Douglas Brundage:** marketing, brand, culture, and creative strategy.
- **Austin and Yash:** Growth and go-to-market.
- **Natalia Karbasova and Mike Knoop:** AI transformation and Consulting.
- **Kieran Klaassen and Willie Stillwell:** engineering and technical practice.

The spokesperson roster supports Dan. It does not dilute his lead role.

## Media priorities

- Business and mainstream: Bloomberg, The Wall Street Journal, Financial Times, The New
  York Times, CNBC, and Fortune.
- Technology and AI: Wired, The Verge, Axios, Semafor, Fast Company, Business Insider,
  and TechCrunch.
- High-trust shows and newsletters where long-form expertise matters.
- Specialist engineering, Consulting, design, marketing, and media outlets matched to
  the spokesperson.

Maintain a named reporter list by beat, relationship, and story fit. Pitch individually.

## Operating cadence

- Run a short weekly press desk to select timely stories and owners.
- Maintain same-day expert availability for rapid response when schedules permit.
- Keep a source bank of company facts, approved claims, research, customer proof,
  biographies, and points of view.
- Brief priority reporters between launches.
- Use smaller updates to feed commentary, owned Editorial, podcasts, and social. Do not
  force each update into a standalone announcement.

## Partnerships

Pursue sponsor-funded reports, shows, events, or physical experiences when the format
strengthens Every's authority. Every retains its point of view, labels the commercial
relationship, and protects Editorial independence.

## Measurement

Report quarterly:

- **Authority:** outlet quality, prominence, message pull-through, repeat relationships,
  expert invitations, and citations.
- **Attention:** estimated impressions, branded search, direct traffic, social sharing,
  and Fame Score movement.
- **Acquisition:** press-attributed free and paid subscribers, conversion, retention,
  and fully loaded CAC when tracking permits.
- **Business:** Consulting inquiries, qualified opportunities, influenced pipeline, and
  partner interest.

Never convert coverage into fictional advertising value.
