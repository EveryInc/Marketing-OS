# Visual Identity System

## Core components

### Brandmark and wordmark

Define construction, meaning, responsive versions, minimum sizes, one-color behavior,
endorsement relationships, and motion potential. A new wordmark may be the distinctive
asset even when the master-brand typography remains.

### Type hierarchy

Specify display, reading, information, label, caption, and interface roles. Define scale,
spacing, alignment, and failure modes. Typography should carry the strategy before images
arrive.

### Color

Define foundation, anchor, accent, information, and accessibility roles. Include digital
values, print conversion requirements, contrast pairs, proportion, and prohibited effects.

### Composition

Define grids, margins, alignment, density, layering, symmetry, disruption, crop, and
negative space. Use two or three named layout modes when they clarify repeated behavior.

### Image system

Specify photography, illustration, collage, diagrams, generative imagery, and when each
is appropriate. Define recurring subject, point of view, crop, light, material, texture,
and postproduction rules.

### Glyphs and sigils

Add only when the system needs indexing, navigation, participation, certification, or a
portable distinctive asset. A sigil is supporting architecture unless explicitly chosen
as the primary mark.

### Brand world

Define the conceptual environment the identity implies. It may support film, projection,
events, packaging, or campaigns. Keep everyday identity simple enough to operate without
the richest world-building layer.

### Motion

Connect motion to the governing idea. Define entrances, transitions, transformations,
tempo, and reduced-motion behavior. Avoid atmospheric motion without communicative work.

## Surface proof

Test the system on:

- Website hero, navigation, content card, and mobile screen
- Editorial or document cover and long-form page
- Email and social
- Product or service interface
- Presentation and sales document
- Print and OOH
- Merchandise or packaging where relevant
- Stage, wayfinding, or environmental application where relevant

The system passes when the surfaces feel related without repeating one composition.
