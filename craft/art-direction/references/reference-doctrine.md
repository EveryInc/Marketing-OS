# Reference Doctrine

## Required human contribution

The human must supply five to 10 references and at least three anti-references. Guide them
with questions:

- What should the identity demonstrate visually?
- Which image, object, book, artist, movement, place, ritual, interface, or artifact feels
  connected for a reason?
- What feels new in this category?
- What has the category copied until it became wallpaper?
- Which reference has an implicit system, not one beautiful surface?
- What should the work never resemble, even if it looks cool?

## Strong references

Strong references are:

- New in the category where they will appear
- Connected to the strategy, product, name, or customer experience
- Specific enough to study
- Capable of generating a system
- Culturally alive without chasing a trend
- Productive when combined with another reference domain

A familiar code can become useful in an unexpected category. Eighties nostalgia is tired
in music and apparel but helped sunscreen and sunglasses brands build coherent worlds.
Heavy-metal codes became revelatory in bottled water because the name, product, and tone
supported them.

Promising underused territories include the American Arts and Crafts movement, Viennese
Expressionism, Soviet jazz posters, government challenge coins and patches, specific
esoteric systems, the memex, alchemy, theaters of memory, and other scientific-
philosophical hybrids. These are starting points, not approved aesthetics.

## Lazy references

- Bauhaus, Apple, and Braun as visual direction
- Generic vintage editorial
- Paul Rand, Milton Glaser, or Warhol without a narrower reason
- “Graffiti” without era, city, surface, hand style, and practitioner
- Fauvism or Matisse cutouts as default illustration
- Generic AI surrealism, chrome, gradients, glowing orbs, and nature-tech collage

The problem is rarely the historical source itself. The problem is arriving at a category
with the same broad reference everyone else used.

## Reference analysis

For every selected reference, record:

- What it contributes
- Which formal codes matter
- Which meaning those codes carry
- Why the codes are fresh in this category
- What must not be copied literally
- Which other reference it combines with
- Which identity components it could inform

Reject references that cannot answer those questions.
