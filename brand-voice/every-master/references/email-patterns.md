# Every email voice patterns

Evidence base: a qualitative review of recent emails delivered from `hello@every.to`,
including editorial newsletters, product and membership launches, objection-handling
follow-ups, and subscriber event invitations through July 19, 2026. The messages were
sent through Kit, but Kit performance analytics were not available. These are observed
voice patterns, not claims about opens, clicks, or conversion.

Use the relevant `craft/email/*` skill for audience, structure, length, CTA format, and
sequence mechanics.

## Patterns that travel across email types

- **The subject carries a thesis or useful object.** Recent examples name the change,
  argument, or artifact directly: a model assessment, a build case study, a practical
  workflow, a product, or an invitation.
- **The preheader adds information.** Editorial sends often use `Plus:` to name the
  secondary stack. Standalone sends use one concrete elaboration.
- **The first paragraph does work.** It states the change, the reader's question, the
  invitation, or the new access. It does not warm up.
- **Specific proof replaces adjectives.** Tools used, money included, time spent, output
  shipped, named practitioners, dates, and logistics carry the claim.
- **Every speaks from practice.** `We built`, `we use`, `we tested`, and a named person's
  firsthand observation are recurring sources of authority.
- **The CTA names the action.** Join, upgrade, RSVP, read, try, or claim. The copy does not
  ask the reader to “learn more.”
- **The tone stays calm.** Even time-bound offers and model news avoid panic and inflated
  excitement.

## Editorial sends

Use Lucid as the base, with Wry or Intimate when the writer's experience earns it.

- Lead with the signal and consequence.
- Build the argument through examples, numbers, and named cases.
- Connect a technical observation to the reader's work or a larger economic shift.
- Admit caveats that change the conclusion.
- Use a compact summary stack when the send contains several pieces.
- Keep wit observational and subordinate to the analysis.

The email may carry a full article or a short shell around one. Follow the current
channel skill and brief; do not infer the format from voice alone.

## Product and membership launches

Use Kinetic + Lucid.

- Say what launched and for whom immediately.
- State the headline benefit with exact numbers and entitlement language.
- Explain origin through a firsthand problem or build story.
- Show the object: concrete products, credits, features, access, or workflow.
- Use a personal aside only when it supplies proof or character.
- Put eligibility, pricing, timing, and upgrade mechanics in plain language.

Avoid making scale or novelty do the persuasive work. The useful object and Every's
firsthand use should carry the pitch.

## Objection-handling and nurture

Use Lucid with a light Intimate register.

- Name the actual question in the reader's language.
- Answer it completely in the next paragraph.
- State conditions and limits plainly.
- Quantify the comparison when the numbers are known.
- Give the reader control: what they can choose, claim, redeem, or ignore.
- Close with one action and a genuine route to reply.

These messages can be very short. Completeness matters more than theatrical persuasion.

## Events and community invitations

Use Intimate + Lucid.

- Open with the gathering and its atmosphere.
- Name the people the reader will meet in specific human terms.
- Put date, time, location, and access conditions in a scannable block.
- State paid or member eligibility directly.
- Use one RSVP action.
- Close warmly without manufactured urgency.

## Email QC

- Does the subject name a thesis, object, or useful event?
- Does the preheader add information instead of repeating the subject?
- Does the first paragraph state the reader-relevant news?
- Is each major claim supported by a number, example, build, test, or named source?
- Are terms, prices, entitlements, dates, and eligibility exact?
- Is there one primary action?
- Does the message sound calm, useful, and written by someone doing the work?
