# Marketing Portfolio: H2 2026 Through H1 2027

Order programs by their likely ability to create useful new free subscribers with the
current team. Run one high-effort build and one lightweight pilot at a time.

## Near-term acquisition

1. **Instagram:** Every's visual front door for Seekers. Preserve the recurring on-camera
   anchor, editorial franchises, collaborator strategy, and distinct jobs of Reels,
   carousels, Stories, and video.
2. **Everyverse affiliates:** begin with eight to 10 curated external creators,
   newsletter writers, podcasters, educators, and community leaders. Give each partner
   approved positioning, assets, access, and a tracked link; do not pay commission in
   this phase. Open a separate paid-member affiliate cohort only after attribution,
   subscriber quality, and economics hold. Pay a modest share of collected first-year
   revenue after discounts, refunds, and chargebacks.
3. **Every Reading Society:** use philosophy, fiction, science, design, AI nonfiction,
   and serious criticism of technology to turn Every's editorial taste into subscriber
   acquisition and affinity. Preserve the press question: Why is an AI company obsessed
   with books? Readers buy their own books. A Bookshop.org storefront can create
   affiliate revenue, and a small copy pool can follow if the program earns demand.

## Authority and demand

4. **Every Intelligence:** make Every's reports the must-read, must-share source of AI
   insight in company Slack channels. Thesis launches the first Future of Work study.
   Publish the main narrative and essential findings openly, then require a free
   subscription for the detailed data package, charts, executive briefing, or practical
   companion. Preserve research independence, methodology, public findings, and the
   willingness to challenge Every's own *After Automation* thesis when evidence demands
   it.
5. **Employee-led podcasts and YouTube:** expand the public cast beyond Dan through
   distinct practitioner-led shows. Dan's show remains the flagship and may become
   *The Every Podcast*. Candidate shows pair Douglas Brundage and Mike Knoop on AI and
   marketing, Austin and Yash on Growth, Natalia Karbasova and Brandon Gell on business,
   and Kieran Klaassen and Willie Stillwell on engineering. Each pairing creates its own
   show. Begin with four-episode pilots, a defined audience, and a subscriber path. These
   pairings remain proposals until the participants and company approve them.

## Conditional 2027 bet

6. **The Compound:** a temporary AI-native office in San Francisco or New York, styled
   through a 1960s Madison Avenue material world of wood, chrome, paper, typography,
   local coffee, and cocktails. Guests book practical workshops and office hours with
   Every practitioners, use a small coworking area during the day, and attend
   conversations with AI leaders at night. The Every Agent appears only where it
   improves the lesson. Every proceeds after the Agent clears an adoption threshold and
   a sponsor covers an agreed share of external costs without taking creative control.
   Entry requires a free subscription. A separate Economist-style OOH campaign drives
   free subscriptions and makes the event public.

Every program starts with an audience, hypothesis, subscriber or Consulting path, direct
and fully loaded cost, decision date, and scale, revise, or stop rule.
