## Every (master brand)

Every uses four behavioral audience segments. They are not demographic personas and do
not create four separate brands. The company promise remains consistent; the proof,
entry point, and desired movement change.

### Builders

People actively building AI products, systems, workflows, or companies. They value
technical credibility, useful artifacts, original ideas, and a creative standard worth
identifying with. Marketing deepens affinity, sharing, membership value, and paid
conversion.

### Operators

People applying AI inside a role, team, or organization. They want practical workflows,
credible peers, and evidence that helps them work differently. Marketing deepens
affinity and paid conversion by making Every useful, distinctive, and culturally
valuable.

### Seekers

People who know AI matters but have not yet developed an AI-native practice. They need a
clear gateway, accessible entry points, and a reason to begin now. Marketing expands
awareness and turns discovery into free subscriptions. Over time, Seekers can become
Builders or Operators.

### Executives

Decision-makers responsible for organizational adaptation. They need authority,
proprietary evidence, applied expertise, and visible client proof. Marketing expands
awareness and creates Consulting demand through research, public relations, events, and
expert visibility.

## Cross-brand audience overlaps

- Builders and Operators form Every's current core and are more likely to know the brand.
- Seekers are the largest underdeveloped awareness and free-subscriber opportunity.
- Executives overlap with Operators but enter through authority, business evidence, and
  Consulting rather than membership alone.
- Product-level audience definitions refine this model without replacing it.
