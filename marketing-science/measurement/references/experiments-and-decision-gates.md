# Experiments and Decision Gates

Use a control only when the channel mechanics, scale, and sample can answer the question.

## Control-test basics

A control group does not receive the campaign. A treatment group does. The difference in
subscription or conversion rates estimates lift.

Credible designs include:

- Randomly assigning comparable partners to launch waves.
- Holding paid media away from a randomly selected audience.
- Comparing treated and untreated geographic markets only when the markets are genuinely
  comparable and the campaign is large enough.

A staggered rollout without randomization does not prove causality. Organic Instagram,
PR, podcasts, and small campaigns usually rely on attribution and cohort comparison.

Growth confirms control design and minimum sample size before launch.

## Decision gates

### Scale

The program clears its primary target, attracts a healthy cohort or creates the declared
authority outcome, and the team can repeat it at a sustainable cost.

### Revise

The result is promising, but one identifiable problem requires another bounded test:
message, offer, partner mix, landing page, cadence, production, or cost.

### Stop

The program misses its target twice, produces weak subscriber quality, requires
unsustainable effort, or cannot state what another test would teach.

## Acquisition versus authority programs

- **Acquisition programs** lead with Marketing-sourced free subscribers, cohort quality,
  CAC, and LTV:CAC.
- **Brand and authority programs** receive a fixed cost and time horizon, a predeclared
  fame or authority outcome, and a subscriber or Consulting path.

Do not force an authority program to pretend it is direct response. Do not allow
"brand" to become an excuse for unlimited cost or undefined success.
