# Attribution and Economics

Use precise language about what Marketing caused and what it touched.

## Subscription claims

- **Attributed:** a tracked Marketing touchpoint connects the campaign to signup. This
  is the default claim.
- **Assisted:** Marketing supplied positioning, creative, PR, a partnership, or the
  campaign idea, but another team or channel received final attribution.
- **Incremental:** a valid control indicates that the campaign caused subscriptions that
  probably would not have happened otherwise. Use this word only when such a test exists.

Attribution describes a recorded path. Incrementality estimates causality.

## Campaign cohorts

Group subscribers by campaign and signup period. Preserve the source on the subscriber
record so a free signup that later converts to paid receives the correct campaign credit.
Follow meaningful cohorts at 30, 90, and 180 days.

## Cost definitions

- **Direct CAC:** cash campaign costs divided by the acquired customers or subscribers
  being evaluated.
- **Fully loaded CAC:** direct costs plus freelance, production, event, partner, tool,
  access, and estimated staff costs.
- **LTV:CAC:** gross-margin lifetime value divided by acquisition cost. Use Growth's
  source-of-truth calculation and state whether CAC is direct or fully loaded.

Do not compare programs using different cost definitions without labeling the difference.

## Minimum tracking

Before launch, create:

- Campaign name and source taxonomy.
- Tagged links, partner codes, event registration, or a dedicated landing page.
- A signup-survey option when tracked paths may be lost.
- Direct and fully loaded cost records.
- The intended free-subscriber, paid, or Consulting path.
- Measurement window and decision date.

When tracking cannot support a causal claim, report attributed results honestly.
