# Compound From Results

Turn completed Marketing work into better future decisions without converting every
campaign result into permanent doctrine.

## Inputs

Start with:

- The approved Program Brief or launch plan.
- The original hypothesis, baseline, target, cost cap, and decision gate.
- The source data and measurement window.
- The campaign cohort, direct costs, and fully loaded costs.
- Relevant audience, fame, authority, and qualitative evidence.

If the original target or baseline is missing, say so. Do not invent a post-hoc success
threshold.

## Workflow

1. **Restate the bet.** Name the audience, intended behavior, primary metric, target,
   cost, and decision date.
2. **Report the result.** Separate observed facts from interpretation. Label subscription
   claims attributed, assisted, or incremental.
3. **Check quality and economics.** Follow meaningful cohorts through conversion,
   retention, revenue, CAC, and LTV:CAC when mature enough.
4. **Explain the result.** Use diagnostic signals, audience composition, creative
   performance, channel behavior, execution quality, and external conditions. Name
   confounds and missing data.
5. **Make the program decision.** Choose scale, revise, stop, or continue measuring.
   Name the owner, next action, cost cap, and next decision date.
6. **Classify the learning.** Put each lesson in one of three levels:
   - **Result record:** true of this campaign or cohort.
   - **Program guidance:** useful for the next iteration of this program or channel.
   - **Durable doctrine:** a stable rule supported by repeated evidence, a strong causal
     test, or an explicit company decision.
7. **Update the narrowest source.** Change only the file governed by the learning. Keep
   unresolved or one-off findings in the campaign's dated result record.

## Routing durable updates

| Learning | Update |
|---|---|
| Audience behavior or segment definition | `marketing-science/archetyping/references/every-audiences.md` |
| Attribution, CAC, cohort, or Fame Score method | `marketing-science/measurement/` |
| Company promise or audience movement | `positioning/every-master/` |
| Channel mechanism or format | The relevant `craft/` channel reference |
| Program sequencing or investment gate | `strategy/program-brief/references/portfolio-rules.md` |
| Editing, voice, or creative pattern | The narrowest `craft/editing/` or approved brand-voice reference |
| Time-bound priority | The current dated portfolio or program plan |

## Promotion standard

Promote a result into durable doctrine only when at least one condition holds:

- The pattern appears in two or more independent programs or cohorts.
- A credible control or experiment supports causality.
- Douglas or company leadership explicitly establishes the rule.
- The result corrects a factual error in the current system.

One surprising result creates a hypothesis. It does not create a universal rule.

## Guardrails

- Do not call correlation causation.
- Do not change the target after seeing the result.
- Do not hide weak economics behind reach or engagement.
- Do not erase a strong creative idea because execution was weak.
- Do not preserve an expensive program because the idea is culturally attractive.
- Do not turn every edit into doctrine; keep the result record intact.
