# The Every Fame Score

The Fame Score is a directional quarterly measure of whether Every is becoming better
known, more clearly understood, and more desired among the audiences it wants to serve.
It complements subscriber economics; it does not replace them.

## Components

1. **Awareness:** Have people heard of Every? Which companies or publications come to
   mind for learning how to use AI at work?
2. **Meaning:** What do people believe Every is? Does "Every makes you AI native" fit?
3. **Consideration:** How likely are people to subscribe, recommend Every, or consider
   Every Consulting?
4. **Affinity:** Among people familiar with Every, does the brand feel useful,
   distinctive, credible, and worth belonging to?

Average the four component percentages into one directional score. Always show the
components beside the total so the score cannot hide the cause of movement.

## Audience cuts

Report Builders, Operators, Seekers, and Executives separately when sample size permits.
Use Clay enrichment to classify role and company where available. Builders and Operators
receive particular attention on affinity and recommendation; Seekers and Executives on
awareness and consideration.

## Survey discipline

- Keep the core questions fixed for one year.
- Use a consistent recruitment mix across subscribers, social followers, event
  attendees, partners, and employee networks.
- Record the source mix every quarter.
- Label the result directional unless the sample is independently recruited to represent
  the target market.
- Do not overinterpret small segment samples or quarter-to-quarter noise.

## Diagnostic signals

Show branded search, direct traffic, repeat visitors, social sharing, inbound press,
speaking requests, and Consulting interest beside the score. These help explain movement
but do not enter the formula.
