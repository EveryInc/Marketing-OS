# Quarterly Marketing Scorecard

Marketing reports one decision-ready scorecard each quarter. The system takes hours, not
weeks, and never becomes a separate research program.

## Required sections

### 1. What Marketing sourced

- Marketing-sourced free subscribers by campaign and channel.
- Paid subscribers when directly attributable.
- Direct campaign cost and fully loaded cost.

### 2. Who joined

Use Clay-enriched data when available:

- Every audience segment.
- Role and seniority.
- Company, industry, and company size.
- Geography.

Report aggregate patterns. Do not expose individual profiles.

### 3. What the cohort became worth

For meaningful campaign cohorts, report at 30, 90, and 180 days:

- Free-to-paid conversion.
- Free and paid retention.
- Revenue and gross-margin lifetime value.
- Direct CAC and fully loaded CAC.
- LTV:CAC when the cohort is mature enough.

Growth supplies the source-of-truth commercial calculations. Marketing interprets what
they mean for the program.

### 4. Whether the brand moved

- Fame Score and its four components.
- Branded search and direct traffic.
- Repeat visitors, social sharing, and inbound press or speaking requests.
- Meaningful press, message pull-through, and repeat journalist demand.

### 5. What happens next

Name what Marketing will scale, revise, and stop. Do not hide an inconclusive result
inside a larger dashboard.

## Readout sentence

> Marketing sourced [X] free subscribers. [Y] converted to paid. The strongest cohort
> came from [Z] and over-indexed among [audience]. Direct CAC was [A]; fully loaded CAC
> was [B]. The Fame Score moved from [C] to [D], led by [component]. PR or research
> produced [E] qualified opportunities. Next quarter Marketing will scale [F], revise
> [G], and stop [H].

## Target setting

Use the first clean quarter to establish baselines. Set the next quarter's targets before
work begins. Never change a target after results arrive.
