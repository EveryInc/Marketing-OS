# Positioning Matrices

## Requirement

Every compound-brand competitive audit includes a positioning matrix. The matrix does not
decide the position by itself; it forces explicit comparison and reveals how sensitive the
analysis is to the chosen frame.

## Generate axes

Produce four or five candidate axis pairs. Good axes:

- Matter to customer choice or perception
- Separate competitors using observable evidence
- Describe independent dimensions
- Avoid moralized good-versus-bad poles
- Avoid simply restating price and quality
- Place the client's brand without rigging the map
- Reveal a strategic tradeoff or white space

For each pair, explain why it matters, the evidence behind it, expected placements, what it
reveals, and what it hides.

## Human gate

The human must choose, edit, or reject the axes before the final matrix is used to lock
positioning. AI often chooses elegant labels that do not match real customer perception.
Treat the labels as a decision, not chart decoration.

## Output

- Four or five axis-pair options
- Recommended pair
- Human ruling
- Competitor placements with evidence
- White-space interpretation
- Sensitivity note using the strongest alternative pair

Avoid false precision. If placement is uncertain, show a range or confidence marker.
