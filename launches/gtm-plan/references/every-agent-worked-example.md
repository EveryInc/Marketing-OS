# Every Agent GTM — Worked Example

Current ruled strategy from the Every Agent GTM working sessions through July 18, 2026.
Use this as a product-specific example of `launches/gtm-plan`, not as a generic template.
Product claims were confirmed by Douglas; market claims still route through the dated
evidence bank and claims clearance.

## Launch facts

- **Public launch:** Tuesday, Aug. 18, 2026
- **Primary audience:** Operators at 10–50-person, Slack-native, AI-forward companies
- **Economic buyer:** Founder, CEO, or COO
- **Champion/admin:** AI-forward Operator or Builder
- **Daily users:** the Slack-based team
- **Launch target:** net-new company-agent users, not Viktor or Claude Tag switchers
- **Secondary audience:** individuals running consultancies, agencies, and other small
  businesses

## The market tension

The first wave of workplace AI made individuals faster. It has done much less for
companies. Useful context remains scattered across people, apps, and private AI sessions.
The person with the most context becomes the human API holding the operation together.

Every Agent turns fragmented AI use into a shared company capability that compounds.

## Positioning for GTM

### Framing

Every is an applied AI lab that builds new ways of working and turns them into software.
Every Agent is the agent Every runs on. It lives in Slack, works across the team's tools,
and becomes more specific to the company through use.

### Primary promise

**Compound your company.**

Every Agent turns repeatable work, corrections, decisions, and context into company
capability the team can use again.

### Supporting advantages

- **Provenance:** It comes from Every with the team's editorial judgment, skills,
  AI-native workflows, software, and, for eligible plans, Every's editorial archive.
- **AI education:** It brings Every's current thinking about AI into the company's work,
  helping the team learn how to operate more AI-natively while using the product.
- **The Every bundle:** Every products such as Spiral and Monologue expand what the agent
  can do inside one system built around the company.

### Functional proposition

The work gets done with AI. The knowledge stays with your company. The company gets
better at working with AI. The cycle compounds.

Supporting line: **With Every Agent, the next task is better than the last.** Avoid an
absolute such as "always" unless product evidence supports it.

### Emotional value

- You stay in command without staying in the middle.
- The company remembers what your team knows, even when someone is unavailable or leaves.
- You get your attention back for the work only you can do.
- Every Agent brings Every's current thinking into the work, helping the team stay close
  to the edge of AI.
- Each Every product added makes the system more capable and more specific to the company.

### Archetype

Ruler is primary because the emotional payoff is command. Every Agent makes operations
more orderly and governable while keeping the user in control. The expression is
institutional capability that compounds, rather than a manager monitoring workers.

The shadow is rigidity and surveillance. Counter it with visible approvals, adaptation,
trusted-peer behavior, editorial provenance, and clear user control.

### Competitive distinction

- Viktor sells more labor through an "AI hire" frame.
- Claude Tag sells a capable shared teammate and already addresses re-explaining pain.
- Every Agent should own compounding institutional capability: what the company learns
  becomes what the company can do.

Do not claim that memory or control is absent from competitors. Re-check current claims
before external use.

## Campaign hierarchy

- **Platform:** Build a company that compounds.
- **Enemy:** Starting over, or organizational amnesia.
- **Demand:** Make every task improve the next one.
- **Brand payoff:** Your company, compounded.

Working articulation:

> Every day, companies solve problems they've already solved. The weekly report is
> rebuilt. The same decision is explained again. The same correction is made in another
> draft. AI can make each pass faster, but the company still starts from zero.

The campaign should show how completed work leaves the company better prepared for what
comes next. The installation instruction belongs in activation, not in the campaign idea.

## Campaign goals

1. Teach the market what effective agent use looks like.
2. Prove Every Agent through real work completed inside Every.
3. Seed the product with respected Operators, Founders, and Builders.
4. Convert demand through concrete starter workflows demonstrated by Every and seeding
   partners.
5. Publish customer receipts that restart the acquisition loop.

## Proof and distribution

- Publish one verified Every operating receipt and five real internal workflows spanning
  operations, product or engineering, growth, editorial, and marketing.
- Seed 20–25 respected Operators, Founders, and Builders before launch. Help each person
  run one recurring workflow more than once. Target 10 publishable receipts.
- Continue seeding after launch to widen the use-case set and keep credible proof moving.
- Publish receipts that show the request, sources, actions, approvals, artifact, result,
  and what the system retained.
- Develop the sustain franchise **How This Company Works** around customer evidence.

## Editorial

Publish a definitive **Guide to Working With Agents** around launch. The guide should be
valuable without Every Agent and cover selecting work, durable context, finished-result
definitions, permissions, inspection, correction, skills, team adoption, and measurement.
Supporting materials can include a first-workflow worksheet, agent-readiness checklist,
and 30-day adoption plan.

## Activation

The launch offer is: **Install Every Agent. Connect two tools. Give it one real job.**

Select a recurring, cross-tool job that is easy to evaluate and currently depends on one
person. Examples include a weekly operating review, executive catch-up, launch-performance
diagnosis, customer-feedback synthesis, project-risk report, growth postmortem, or
recurring decision brief.

Activation should progress from one completed task to visible Slack output, teammate use,
repeat use, a saved skill or schedule, and evidence of value accumulated.

## Video system

- Branded launch film built around **Build a company that compounds**
- 60–90 second product overview
- A large library of short Slack interactions showing context retrieval, actions,
  approvals, finished artifacts, scheduling, correction-to-skill, teammate use, AI
  education, and Every bundle expansion
- Six complete workflow stories across core functions
- Customer and seeded-user receipts
- Creator kits, onboarding videos, website loops, and horizontal, square, and vertical
  cutdowns at 6, 15, and 30 seconds

The film should make the company-level idea memorable. The interaction library should
prove it repeatedly.

## Dream headline standard

The dream headline should validate the category thesis or company-level outcome. It is a
retro target, not launch copy. Current examples:

- **Every Built an AI Agent That Makes Your Company Smarter Every Time It Works**
- **This Slack Agent Learns How Your Company Works**
- **Every Is Building the Memory Layer for the AI-Native Company**

## Initial measures and dates

- 100 qualified workspace installs in the first 30 days
- Identify activation behaviors that predict paid conversion and retention
- 10 publishable customer receipts and five case-study candidates
- Revenue and paid-conversion targets set after pricing locks
- Organic conversation and earned attention through creator partnerships
- One mainstream press story about Every Agent or Every's broader company story

Reporting moments:

- **Aug. 18:** launch-day dashboard
- **Aug. 25:** week-one review and sustain lock
- **Sept. 17:** 30-day cohort report
- **Oct. 16:** 60-day review and next-quarter recommendation

## Guardrails

- Do not reduce the GTM strategy to dates or a channel checklist.
- Do not treat "Your company, compounded" as sufficient by itself; it is the payoff.
- Do not confuse "install, connect, give it a job" with the campaign idea.
- Do not use weak abstractions such as "companies keep paying for the same work."
- Do not claim Every Agent is the only agent with memory, control, or initiative.
- Do not optimize the launch around low cost before value and differentiation are proven.
