# Curated Event GTM Reference

Use this reference for conferences, salons, summits, retreats, communities, and other launches where admission and audience composition affect the product.

## Classify the experience

Choose the participation mode before designing applications or attendee work:

- **Consumption-led:** the product is the program, learning, entertainment, and room. Attendance is sufficient.
- **Participation-enhanced:** profiles, questions, introductions, or small exercises improve the experience but remain optional or lightweight.
- **Co-created:** workshops, critiques, working groups, peer production, or participant artifacts are required to deliver the promise.

Use direct evidence from the brief, customer research, or approved program. An application process, curated room, editorial prompt, event name, or civic metaphor does not make an event co-created.

## Start with the room

Define the room before writing the channel plan:

- The change or decision the event helps a person make
- The primary participant and explicit non-target
- The desired mix by discipline, seniority, geography, customer type, or point of view
- Capacity, paid seats, comps, speakers, sponsors, press, staff, and waitlist reserve
- Price, what it includes, purchase window, refund policy, and transfer rules
- The program, learning, access, or connection that justifies the price
- Any contribution expected before, during, and after the event, only when required by the confirmed participation mode

Use ranges rather than false precision for room composition. Track fit alongside fill rate.

Keep roles separate: applicant, paid attendee, invited attendee, speaker, moderator, commissioned contributor, sponsor, press, and public website participant. One role's obligations, consent, or success measures do not transfer to another role without an explicit program decision.

## Application flow

Keep the first application short. Ask only what is needed to judge fit and form the room:

1. Identity, role, organization, location, and one relevant link
2. Primary discipline or working context
3. The question, decision, or topic bringing them to the event
4. What they hope to understand, experience, or change
5. The people or perspectives they hope to encounter
6. Relevant experience or perspective, when useful for composing the room
7. Membership or customer status, when relevant to admission or price
8. Company reimbursement interest, when a justification letter is available
9. Agreement to the participation and conduct policy

For participation-enhanced or co-created formats, add only the questions needed to prepare the work: the proposed contribution, relevant evidence, materials, collaboration preferences, and permission to share. Do not ask consumption-led attendees to manufacture a proposition, artifact, or public point of view.

Publish selection criteria and response time. Use accepted, waitlisted, and declined states. Give accepted applicants a clear purchase deadline. Do not collect payment from a waitlist.

## Post-acceptance attendee document

Separate any public profile or contribution from private logistics.

### Optional public profile, with field-level consent

- Name, role, organization, and chosen links
- Short question, interest, project, or proposition, depending on the participation mode
- Evidence or earned experience only when relevant
- Topics they can help with
- Topics they want to understand
- Directory and introduction preferences

### Private

- Badge name and optional pronouns
- Arrival and departure plans
- Hotel-rate interest; make clear that interest is not a reservation
- Dietary requirements, allergies, and cross-contact severity
- Accessibility accommodations; ask for needs rather than diagnoses
- Direct contact with the access lead
- Optional emergency contact
- Photography and recording choice
- Legal terms and separate consent choices for public display, directory, introductions, analytics, and model use

An agent may draft answers from sources the attendee approves. The attendee must approve public fields, see what stays private, edit recommendations, and use a manual form if they prefer.

## Matchmaking

Match on the participant’s interests, desired perspective, relevant experience, and contribution when applicable. Explain every suggestion in one sentence. Let the person accept, decline, or change the criteria. Keep a human curator responsible for the final program and sensitive introductions.

Useful measures include attendee-information completion, optional public-profile opt-in, suggested-introduction acceptance, meeting completion, learning value, program quality, and a post-event decision or outcome. Avoid measuring introductions created without evidence that they were useful.

## Concept-inflation gate

Before locking the GTM, trace every distinctive activation through the document. A website prompt, token, editorial franchise, agentic document, or experiential device may support the event without defining it.

Stop and correct the plan when an unconfirmed activation appears in two or more of these places:

- The event promise or reason to buy
- Selection criteria
- Required attendee preparation
- Program architecture
- PR's lead story
- Primary success measures

The repetition is a drift signal. Keep the activation subordinate unless direct evidence says customers buy the event for it or the program cannot deliver value without it.

## Accessibility and privacy gate

Before launch, confirm:

- Named accessibility and privacy owners
- Public access commitments and contact path
- Venue access, seating, captioning, quiet space, dietary process, and service-animal policy
- Data retention and deletion rules
- No use of accommodation details in admission decisions
- No model training on attendee data unless the person gives specific, informed consent
- A tested manual fallback for every agentic step

## Competitive benchmark method

Research four to six events with roles:

1. The largest first-year breakout
2. The closest transferable first-year analog
3. A mature editorial or community extension
4. A strong application-based room
5. An experiential or place-led example, if relevant
6. A cautionary example when a common tactic failed

For each, capture the first-edition date, result, audience, offer, distribution engine, partner jobs, proof, first value or behavior, press mechanism, and what happened next. Prefer first-party histories and contemporaneous reporting. Cite every number.

End with four judgments:

- Which launch popped by scale?
- Which model is most transferable and why?
- What mechanism should this launch borrow?
- What should it reject despite surface similarity?

## Event scorecard

Choose only the measures that guide a decision:

- Qualified applications and source
- Acceptance rate and room composition
- Accepted-to-paid conversion
- Revenue and capacity by seat type
- Application and attendee-document completion
- Preparation behavior completed, only when the participation mode requires one
- Attendance and no-show rate
- Suggested introductions accepted and completed
- Publishable contributions for co-created formats; useful outcomes for all formats
- Referral, renewal, return intent, or membership retention
- Earned coverage that repeats the intended market belief

Every measure needs a review date and a decision it can change.
