# Deliverable Templates

Structural templates for each major launch deliverable. Copy the relevant template and
populate it for the launch at hand.

## Social brief

```markdown
# [Product] [Launch Type] — Social Brief

**Launch date:** [date]
**What's launching:** [one sentence]
**Goal:** [what success looks like]
**CTA:** [action] → [URL]

---

## Why This Launch Matters

[Two to three sentences. The story, not the changelog.]

---

## Messaging Architecture

### Primary angle: [headline claim]

[One to two sentences explaining the angle.]

### Secondary angle: [headline claim]

[One to two sentences.]

### Tertiary angle: [headline claim]

[One to two sentences.]

---

## Key Product Proof Points

[Bullet list of specific, credible data points.]

---

## Social Proof to Feature

| Person | Role | Quote | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|

---

## Asset Needs

| Asset | What it shows | Used on | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|

---
```

## Email options

```markdown
# [Product] [Launch Type] — Email Options

[Context sentence.] Three options with different lead angles. All follow Every email
craft rules: product launch mode, 250–400 words, declarative subjects, no hype words,
action-verb CTAs.

Audience: [segment] (sent from [address] via Kit).

---

## Option A: [Lead angle description]

**Subject:** [declarative, no question marks]
**Preheader:** [extends subject, does not repeat it]

Hi {{ subscriber.first_name | default: "There" }},

[Body — 250–400 words, bold-label bullets for features, single CTA.]

→ [Action verb CTA text]

—[Sign-off]

---

## Option B: [Lead angle description]

[Same structure.]

---

## Option C: [Lead angle description]

[Same structure.]

---

## Recommendation

[Which option works best for which audience, and why.]
```

## Positioning brief

```markdown
# [Product] — Positioning & Research Brief

## 1. Positioning One-Pager

**Positioning statement:** [one sentence]
**Category language:** "[term]" (not "[rejected terms]")
**Core promise:** [user benefit in one line]
**Headline direction:** [working headline]

**Target audience:**
- Primary: [who]
- Secondary: [who]

**Product moments:** [three to five key use cases]

**Competitive differentiation:** [what we do that others do not]

**Guardrails:** [what we do not say, what we avoid]

---

## 2. Research One-Pager

**Customer profile:** [backed by evidence]
- [testimonial 1]
- [testimonial 2]

**Competitive landscape:**

| Competitor | Positioning | Strengths | Gaps |
|---|---|---|---|

**Market dynamics:** [what is changing]

**Breakthrough strategy:** [how we win]

---

## 3. Best-Practice Copy Flow

| Section | Position | Psychological job | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hero | Above fold | Category + promise | [specific recommendation] |
| Social proof | Below hero | Trust | [specific recommendation] |
| Features | Mid-page | Evidence | [specific recommendation] |
| Use cases | Mid-page | Identification | [specific recommendation] |
| Pricing | Lower page | Commitment | [specific recommendation] |
| Closing CTA | Bottom | Action | [specific recommendation] |

---

## 4. Current Site Audit

[Section-by-section analysis against the positioning brief.]
[Priority fixes P0–P3 with impact and effort.]
```
